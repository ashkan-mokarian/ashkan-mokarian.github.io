ABOUT

I am a master student and scholarship holder in the IMPRS-CS graduate program since october 2013. In the winter of 2013 and spring of 2014, I was a research intern at Combinatorial Image Analysis group, where I worked with Bjoern Andres. During the first two semesters, I managed to successfully satisfy the degree requirements in terms of theoretical background (usually this phase takes at least three semesters), by taking core courses like Optimization, Convex Optimization, Probabilistic Graphical Models, and Randomized Algorithms among others. With this in mind, I am planning to focus more on research and do my master's thesis at Machine Learning Group under the advising of Matthias Hein. I received my B.Sc. degree in Electrical Engineering (Systems Theory and Control Engineering) from the School of Electrical and Computer Engineering at University of Tehran.

I am interested in Optimization and Machine Learning, specially in Large-Scales. In general, I find it interesting to use Optimization as a mathematical toolset, Statistics to model and obtain real world models, Probabilistic methods to explore the structures, and Approximation to make real world problems solvable. I enjoy thinking about problems in Combinatorics and graph theory. I like to design and implement algorithms in Machine Vision, since the outputs are more interesting to look at, moreover, huge and large-scale empirical datas are available !!!
